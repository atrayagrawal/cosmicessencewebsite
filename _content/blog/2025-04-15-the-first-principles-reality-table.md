# The First Principles Reality Table

When we think about problems, we often start with assumptions. We assume things are a certain way because that's how they've always been, or because that's what everyone says.

But what if we stripped all that away?

## The Exercise

The First Principles Reality Table is a simple framework for breaking down complex problems into their fundamental truths.

### Step 1: Write down your problem

Be specific. "I want to start a business" is too vague. "I want to help students learn physics better" is better.

### Step 2: List all your assumptions

What are you assuming about:
- Your customers?
- Your product?
- The market?
- The problem itself?

### Step 3: For each assumption, ask: Is this actually true?

Go through each one. Can you prove it? Or is it just something you believe?

### Step 4: Build from what you know is true

Now you're thinking from first principles. This is where innovation happens.

## Example

**Assumption:** Students need more practice problems.

**Is this true?** Maybe. But what if they actually need better explanations? Or more confidence? Or different formats?

**Reality:** We don't know until we ask them.
